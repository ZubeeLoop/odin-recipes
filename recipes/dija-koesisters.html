Dija Koesisters

Traditional Cape Malay Koesister recipe

This traditional Cape Malay pastry or Koesister, often described as a spicy, sweet donut with a cake like texture that is fried in oil and then rolled in syrup and finished off with a sprinkle of coconut.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 4 cups of cake flour
  • 10 grams of instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 250g of butter
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tbsp fine cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp of ground cardamom seeds
  • 1 tbsp fine ginger
  • 1 tbsp of dried, grounded Mandarin orange peel
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 & 1/2 cups of lukewarm water

SUGAR SYRUP:

  • 3 cups of sugar
  • 3 cups of water

STEPS:

  1. In a large bowl, mix all the dried ingredients together
  2. Add butter, mixing it through the dried ingredients
  3. Add 1 large egg and lukewarm water
  4. Knead the dough for 10 minutes
  5. Cover with a tea towel and allow to rise for abut 90 minutes in a warm and dark area of your kitchen
  6. Once dough has risen completely, using your hands, shape little oblong shapes and place on floured surface
  7. Deep fry your oblong shapes in oil on a medium to high heat, until brown on both sides
  8. To make the syrup, boil the water and sugar until bubbling
  9. Dip the Koesisters into the warm sugar syrup and soak for a minute
  10. Place Koesisters on a plate and sprinkle fine desiccated coconut
